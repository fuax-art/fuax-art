About

Two years ago I was living the best days of my life: a third-floor apartment overlooking a park. My daughter and I. Endless possibilities.

Then I got fired. Five years of dedication, gone over a phone call. Everyone involved knows it was bullshit.

Fell behind on rent. Family chaos hit—my brother needed help and moved in with me.

I fell back into the old ways: forgery, laundering, trap work. The whole thing.

Fast Forward

I'm squatting for a year. Officially wanted. Cops light me up nine times—punch it every time.

Never got me.

Prison's coming

My road dog gets hemmed up. Homelessness is coming. I'm watching it all crumble.

But my daughter still looks at me like I'm unstoppable. Like she's the luckiest girl alive.

All I see in the mirror is me letting her down.

One night she walks into my room —

— past the dirty laundry, torn electronics, debris of a canvas I'd destroyed because I hated the man in the mirror.

She lights up with joy. So do I.

We're on OpenSea, browsing collections. I'm trying to explain NFTs with my limited knowledge.

We come across CryptoPunks. She asks: "Dad, what are Cyber Pugs?"

She loves pugs. I thought it was hilarious.

Suddenly, a spark

For the first time in months, I felt hope.

I'm gonna make it there. One way or another.

World's okayest dad.

At least I'm near my kid.

Not scared of prison; been there, done that—different story for a different time.

I gotta leave her something. Something to remember me by.

CyberPugs

Back of my broken SUV: a busted laptop I had to Frankenstein into a monitor—Sketch, GIMP, Krita, crumpled paper, sketchbooks.

I made her a sketchbook. It explained everything:

My process

How I feel inside

Why I had to go away

What I did

Blunt, raw honesty is kinda my thing.
